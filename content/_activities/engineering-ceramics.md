# Engineering Ceramics: Advanced Structural Ceramics with Flying Faculty Prof. Michael Scheffler

The Master of Science in Materials Science (MSI) program at **Vietnamese-German University (VGU)** was honored to welcome **Prof. Dr. rer. nat. Michael Scheffler**, Head of the Chair of Non-Metallic Materials at **Otto von Guericke University Magdeburg (OVGU)**, Germany, to deliver the advanced *Engineering Ceramics* module.

As one of the most critical classes of structural and functional materials in modern technology, high-performance ceramics are highly valued for their extreme temperature resistance, superior hardness, exceptional chemical stability, and wear resistance. They are the backbone of aerospace turbines, high-speed automotive brakes, deep-sea drills, and biomedical implants.

---

## 🔬 Core Learning Pillars: Inside the Ceramics Module

Under the direct supervision of Prof. Scheffler, MSI students dive deep into the microstructural engineering of these robust non-metallic systems:

- **Atomic Bonding & Crystal Structures:** Understanding the underlying ionic and covalent bonds that give ceramics their unmatched hardness and thermal stability.
- **Powder Processing & Sintering Science:** Exploring industrial powder synthesis, green compact shaping, and high-temperature sintering mechanisms to eliminate porosity.
- **Microstructure-Property Mechanics:** Correlating grain boundaries and phase distributions with mechanical and thermal performance.
- **Fracture Mechanics & Reliability:** Mastering mathematical models of ceramic brittle fracture and Weibull statistics to design fail-safe structural elements.
- **Functional Ceramic Systems:** Studying electrical, magnetic, and optical ceramic applications including solid oxide fuel cells (SOFCs) and advanced sensors.
